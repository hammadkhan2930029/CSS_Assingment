The Ocean Taught Me About A Broken Heart
I carried my broken heart to the ocean shore to cast into the water and let drift forevermore the gentle winds spoke about the beauty there as seabird's noisy calls resounded thru the air waves lapped the beach in its timeless destiny the sky was full of colors painted just for me watching the golden sun slip into the twilight still I remained pondering through the night silvery moonlight danced over windswept seas and reminded me the tides changed constantly my problems vanished in the vastness of it all compared to the ocean my broken heart was small....

On The Death Of A Favourite Cat Drowned In A Tub Of Gold Fishes
Twas on a lofty vase's side, Where China's gayest art had dyed The azure flowers that blow; Demurest of the tabby kind, The pensive Selima, reclined, Gazed on the lake below. Her conscious tail her joy declared; The fair round face, the snowy beard, The velvet of her paws, Her coat, that with the tortoise vies, Her ears of jet, and emerald eyes, She saw; and purred applause. Still had she gazed; but 'midst the tide Two angel forms were seen to glide, The genii of the stream: Their scaly armor's Tyrian hue Through richest purple to the view Betrayed a golden gleam. A frightening lullaby
Little buds raised their heads from their mothers womb but spring like a reluctant bride stayed back at the gate. Let not the buds to bloom Snow white jasmines and lilies showering their blessings from sky The buds all in frenzy mood. Showing their gratitude swinging and smiling in full moon silver night, whistled the wind a scarry mournful tune. Began to recite a frightening lullaby out side the door, I pulled my quilt over head said my prayers and. Began snoring like a wild boar when milk man rang the bell I opened the doors ; saw dry, leaves between mosquito net and window sill.
